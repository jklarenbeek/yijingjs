The Ching and Boolean Argebra

The I Ching, or Book of Changes, is the most widely read of the five Chinese Classics. The book was traditionally written by the legendary Chinese Emperor Fu Hsi (2953-2838 B.C.). It is possible that the the I Ching originated from a prehistoric divination technique which dates back as far as 5000 B.C. Thus it may be the oldest text at this site. Futher commentaries were added by King Wen and the Duke of Chou in the eleventh century B.C. Also a nice little fact, this sequence in western science is also called a binary sequence. One can calculate any hexagram in the Fu Hsi sequence into a decimal number by iterating each line as a index from top to bottom -starting at line 0 and ending at line 5-
